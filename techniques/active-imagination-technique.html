Active Imagination (Dream Work Technique) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Consciously dialoguing with dream figures by placing them in a chair, writing in stream-of-consciousness, or moving expressively—as a way of receiving unconscious messages from dream content.
Instrumental Use

Primary: establish communication with unconscious content from dreams. Ask the dream figure questions; receive surprising answers from the unconscious. Determine whether the figure is subjective (represents a part of self) or objective (represents another person in life). Use in combination with Imaging: create the figure through Imaging Sensations while dialoguing, making them more tangible and increasing unconscious access.

Craft Use

Can be used to deepen a dream-based choice: active imagination with the key symbols of the dream builds a richer, more tangible inner reality for the character. Particularly effective when combined with Symbolic Imaging.

When to Use

After recording and re-reading a dream; when the dream contains significant but unclear symbols or figures. Also: when the actor wants to extend the working life of a dream beyond its original imaging.
