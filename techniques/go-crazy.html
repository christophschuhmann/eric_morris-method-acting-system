Go Crazy Both

Freeing the ActorIrreverent Acting
More intense than Silly Dilly. Starts with burst of energy like Abandonment. Combines Silly Dilly, Abandonment, and impulsivity. Unpredictable, unplanned antics using entire stage — leaping, twisting, turning, impulsive sounds, rolling on floor, jumping up, singing, bellowing, screeching, distorting body. Stops when physically exhausted.
Instrumental Use

Addresses fear of embarrassment by going to maximum outrageous behavior. After doing this, any scene obligation seems tame. Builds acceptance of impulsive, unplanned behavior.

Craft Use

Sets up foundation of flamboyance and colorful behavior; good preparation for free-flowing, expressive, uninhibited characters.

When to Use

Before scenes requiring high impulsive expressiveness; when restricted or inhibited; as a pre-scene preparation for volatile or exuberant characters.
