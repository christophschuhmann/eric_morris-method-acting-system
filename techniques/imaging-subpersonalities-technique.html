Imaging to Access Subpersonalities (Four-Part Process) Both

Imaging & Unconscious
A four-step imaging technique for eliciting specific subpersonalities: (1) image the subpart sensorially in a chosen physical space; (2) step into its energy field and inhabit it; (3) use mirror work to verify and deepen embodiment; (4) image through the subpart's perspective toward character obligations.
Instrumental Use

Used instrumentally to develop an aware ego: the actor learns to recognize, access, and hold the various selves in balance. Helps actors become familiar with primary, opposite, and disowned selves as a prerequisite to using subpersonalities as an acting tool.

Craft Use

Used as the choice approach for accessing the subpersonality that matches the character's dominant energy. The actor images the subpart, inhabits it, and then allows it to carry the performance. Can be done in 8-10 minutes. Any imaging technique (Fragmented, Story, Sensations) can be used within the four-step structure.

When to Use

In preparation before a rehearsal or performance, or during the rehearsal period to establish which subpersonality best serves the material. Prerequisite: familiarity with the subpersonality through prior voice dialogue or other approach techniques.
