Period Research for Character World-Building Craft

Hollywood Stories
The cast and director immerse themselves in the historical, economic, and cultural context of the period in which the play is set — through recordings, books, images, and cultural artifacts — to create a shared understanding of the world the characters inhabit.
Instrumental Use

Creates a shared imaginative world for all cast members, reducing the artificiality of the rehearsal environment.

Craft Use

Grounds the characters' behavior and concerns in a specific historical reality; ensures that references and motivations that arise from the period are genuinely felt rather than theoretically understood.

When to Use

When directing period plays or any work set in a time substantially different from the present; most effective when done collectively by the whole cast, not just individually.
