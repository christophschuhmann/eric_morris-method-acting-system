Creating an Experience (Emotional Obligation) Craft

Imaging & Unconscious
The actor selects a personal past experience that produced the needed emotions and uses Imaging to re-enter that experience sensorially, reliving its emotional impact during the scene.
Instrumental Use

Not primarily instrumental in use.

Craft Use

Used for the emotional obligation. The actor identifies a past event with the right emotional quality and images it in preparation or during the scene. Example: actress in 'Hearts' who must experience terror of falling uses her real memory of a motorcycle accident, imaging the specific sensory details to re-experience the terror.

When to Use

When the actor has a personal experience with the exact emotional quality needed. Works especially well when the experience is viscerally remembered with strong sensory detail.
