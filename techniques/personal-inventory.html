Personal Inventory Both

Being and DoingFreeing the ActorNo Acting Please
Always begins with the question 'How do I feel?' and waits for, explores, and follows the response. Used at the start of every class exercise, scene, or monologue to identify the actor's present emotional state. For impressive blocks: done out loud or in front of group to discover incubated feelings. Morris: 'How do I feel? I feel tense... I feel a little frustrated... I feel blocked...'
Instrumental Use

Identifies the actor's actual present emotional state before any acting work begins — ensures starting from identifiable reality. For emotional insecurities: helps discover what is getting in the way of experiencing impulses and feelings.

Craft Use

Used during scene work when the actor drifts into imposition or loses touch with organic impulses. Taking Personal Inventory mid-scene redirects to real internal life and stops imposed behavior automatically.

When to Use

Many times daily, always before working, whenever tension or disconnection is felt during a scene, and as first diagnostic step when any acting problem arises.
