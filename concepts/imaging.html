Imaging

Imaging & Unconscious
A structured, sensory-rich mental visualization process in which the actor creates vivid internal experiences — seeing, hearing, smelling, tasting, and feeling — with the purpose of stimulating authentic emotional life, communicating with the unconscious, and fulfilling acting obligations. Choice Approach #26 in Morris's system and his fifth MEGAPPROACH.

Morris distinguishes imaging from casual daydreaming or 'head-only' visualization. Imaging must involve all five senses simultaneously, not just the visual. The image should leave the 'inner screen' and surround the actor as a living reality — the actor is IN the image, not watching it from outside.

Imaging can be used instrumentally (to free the actor's instrument) and as craft (to fulfill scene obligations). When practiced repeatedly, images sink into the unconscious and begin to influence behavior organically. The unconscious 'speaks' in images (Jung: 'Images are the language of the unconscious') — imaging is therefore the primary conduit between conscious craft work and unconscious impulse.

Morris identifies seven imaging techniques: (1) Story Imaging — creating an experience as a sequential narrative; (2) Fragmented Imaging — random, disconnected images; (3) Verbal Imaging — creating imagery through evocative words; (4) Fantasy Imaging — imaginary, non-real scenarios; (5) Imaging with Sense Memory — combining sensory recall with visual imaging; (6) Symbolic Imaging — images that represent abstract realities; (7) Involuntary Imaging — allowing the unconscious to generate images without direction.
