Emotional Obligation

Imaging & UnconsciousIrreverent Acting
One of the seven obligations: what the character feels in every given scene and in every moment throughout the play, and how those emotions change. The emotional life the actor must organically stimulate for themselves through appropriate choices and choice approaches.

The emotional obligation is the most central obligation in most scenes. The actor identifies the emotional obligation, then makes choices to create the realities that promote the emotional experience. Choices must address the full emotional picture — not just stimulate surface emotions without the underlying passion and need that motivate them.

Three primary choice types for emotional obligation (using Imaging as choice approach): (1) Creating a Piece of Music — selecting evocative music and hearing it internally while performing; (2) Creating an Experience — selecting a personal past event that produced the needed emotions and re-entering it; (3) Creating a Person — selecting a real or imagined person who evokes the needed feelings and conjuring them.
