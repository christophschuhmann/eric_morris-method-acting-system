Consciousness

Ultimate ConsciousnessFreeing the ActorHollywood Stories
An expanded state of awareness, curiosity, sensitivity, perception, interest, knowledge, imagination, and concern for all life that an actor cultivates both as a life practice and as an acting prerequisite. Its development creates the bridge to unconscious impulses and enables the actor to recognize and address instrumental obstacles.

Everyone is born with consciousness, but its level depends on upbringing and deliberate development. Its components include: curiosity, imagination, awareness, sensitivity, perception, interest, knowledge, and affinity/concern for life. Each component reinforces the others.

Freeing the Actor identifies five components: Observation, Awareness, Curiosity, Commitment, and Information. Without consciousness, an actor cannot identify what blocks them or how to address it. Most people live with a restricted level of consciousness limited to survival and personal interests. Developing consciousness is an ongoing discipline, not a one-time achievement.

The actor's consciousness (from My Hollywood Stories) refers to the distinctive inner world actors share that non-actors cannot fully understand — the constant uncertainty, rejection, loneliness, and sporadic income of the profession. Two actors who don't know each other can communicate this shared experience wordlessly.
