Affective Memory

Ultimate ConsciousnessIrreverent ActingImaging & Unconscious
A choice approach (approach #12 in Morris's system) in which the actor uses a personally meaningful emotional experience from their own life — recreated sensorially — to stimulate the emotional response required by the scene. Sometimes called 'emotional memory' or 'emotional recall.'

Affective memory requires the actor to go back to a real past experience and recreate it sensorially — not by thinking about it intellectually or by reliving the pain, but by recreating the sensory circumstances that surrounded the emotional event. The emotional response is a byproduct of successful sensory recreation, not of direct emotional effort.

Morris treats affective memory as one of many choice approaches — not the dominant technique that Strasberg made it. It should be used when other approaches fail or when the emotional obligation is extreme and deeply personal. Affective Memory Imaging: the actor first creates the experience visually/kinesthetically through imaging rather than pure sensory recall.
