BEING

Irreverent ActingNo Acting PleaseUltimate ConsciousnessBeing & DoingFreeing the Actor
The foundational state of authentic, unedited presence in which the actor is genuinely affected by internal and external stimuli and responds by expressing feelings on an organic, moment-to-moment basis. BEING is total presence — the acknowledgment, acceptance, and expression of all impulses without intellectual interference, suppression, or social obligation. It is not merely an acting state but a living state that must exist 24 hours a day; the fullest, most real state achievable on stage or in life. BEING BEFORE DOING, BEING BEFORE ACTING. The canvas metaphor: BEING is the canvas; technique is the paint — 90% of training should go to BEING.

Morris developed the concept of BEING after observing that actors were fullest on stage when closest to what they actually felt. The split between what an actor really feels and what they express is the core problem BEING addresses. BEING is contrasted with 'natural' acting, which is the imitation of truth rather than truth itself.

When truly BEING, behavior includes infinite colors, distractions, interruptions, and unpredictable emotional changes. All emotions have equal value — 'Everything is a Number Six.' Suppressing any impulse caps everything. The actor who is truly BEING is unique, individual, and unlike any other person. Conditioning, social taboos, and behavioral restrictions are major obstacles to BEING.

BEING is the bridge between process and application of process. It is the first stage in the chain leading to Ultimate Consciousness (Being → Irreverence → Ultimate Consciousness). Without a solid BEING state, the later stages cannot be reached. When an actor achieves BEING, the work paradoxically becomes more theatrical and meets material demands more imaginatively.

The Being and Doing philosophy (from the book of the same name) captures the essence: in real life, people DO things because of real impulses — genuine BEING causes authentic DOING. Stage acting must replicate this organic process rather than imitating the surface result. Common acting problems — imposition, compensation, dependencies — vanish when the actor is in a genuine BEING state. Being in Every Role: versatility comes from accessing different dimensions of oneself, not from becoming different people.
