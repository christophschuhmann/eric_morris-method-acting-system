Splits

Being & DoingFreeing the ActorNo Acting Please
A disconnection within the actor's instrument in which different components — physical, emotional, intellectual, and vocal — stop functioning as a unified whole, producing incongruence between what is being expressed in one channel and another. 'The instrument's lie detector.' The direct result of imposing behavior rather than responding organically.

Three primary types (Freeing the Actor): (1) Physical-and-emotional split — body appears disconnected from emotional expression, limbs fixed in positions that don't reflect the emotion. (2) Vocal-and-emotional split — the actor manipulates the voice to express emotions not genuinely felt. (3) Intellectual split — the actor functions from the head, directing expression cerebrally. Multiple simultaneous splits are possible (two-, three-, or four-way).

Splits form when the actor short-circuits the natural stimulus-effect-response-expression process by assuming behaviors, imposing attitudes, or acting instead of BEING. One denial sets up a chain reaction of denials until the lack of reality is epidemic. BEING eliminates splits or mends them when they occur. The antidote is Personal Inventory: reconnecting with real feelings and expressing them.
