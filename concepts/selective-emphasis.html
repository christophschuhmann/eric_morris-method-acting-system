Selective Emphasis

Imaging & UnconsciousIrreverent Acting
A technique of focusing attention on and amplifying only the part of an object, person, or stimulus that emotionally affects you, rather than the whole — intensifying the emotional impact by creatively manipulating how you perceive something to make it serve the scene's needs.

Often only part of an object or person affects you; the whole may have a different or diluted impact. Used in conjunction with Available Stimulus, Endowments, Imaging, and many other choice approaches. Creative Manipulation: adjusting how you interpret or relate to an element so it creates the desired response. Example: 'That twinkle in her eyes: every time she looks at me she lights up for me — it's all for me!' Selective Isolation: close focus on a specific element while letting everything else recede.

Also used with Imaging: when only part of a choice is impelling, the actor does not recreate the whole experience but zeros in on the meaningful component. Example: if an obligation requires grief, and the choice is a complex event, the actor selectively emphasizes only the grief-producing moments.
