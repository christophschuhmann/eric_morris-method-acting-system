Preparation

Being & DoingIrreverent ActingNo Acting Please
Everything an actor does to get ready to act — comprising 95% of acting itself. Encompasses both instrumental preparation (freeing the instrument to achieve a BEING state) and craft preparation (working for choices to fulfill scene obligations). The largest part of the acting process.

Preparation is like a continent with many regions. Pre-preparation: getting ready to get ready, using relaxation, sensitizing, and Personal Inventory. Instrumental preparation: bringing the actor to a BEING state by eliminating blocks, fears, and tensions. Craft preparation: specifically addressing and changing emotional states in relation to material.

Eight major categories of preparation (Irreverent Acting): (1) Standard Relaxation Cluster, (2) Involvement Preparations, (3) Emotional Preparations, (4) Ego Preparations, (5) Large Expurgatives, (6) Relationship Preparations, (7) Interim Preparations, (8) Irreverent Preparations. The same exercise used with different emphasis serves either instrumental or craft purposes.

Types in No Acting Please: (1) Instrumental preparations — tension, fear, inhibition; (2) Relational preparations — connecting to people, objects, places; (3) Role preparations — fulfilling the emotional obligations of the material. Must always deal with instrumental problems first. Knowing yourself and your unique instrumental needs is as important as knowing many exercises.
