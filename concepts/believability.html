Believability

Imaging & UnconsciousIrreverent Acting
A choice approach in which the actor cultivates the willingness and ability to believe in imaginary circumstances as if they were real, and mixes real and fictional elements so deftly that they become indistinguishable. Both actors work together to build a shared fictional reality that serves the scene.

Like Imaging, believability hooks into the child in the actor — connecting to the sense of play and pretend. Two actors identify obligations and conflict elements, then structure a Believability improvisation together. A mixture of some truth and a lot of untruth; starts with reality and adds elements to promote the relationship and emotional needs of the scene. The deft mixing of truth and untruth makes them indistinguishable — the actors lose track of what is real and what isn't.

Believability is not a decision ('I will believe this') but an accumulated sensory-emotional state built through process. Cumulative imaging + Believability: as the actor repeatedly images the play's realities, these are absorbed into the unconscious and the willingness to believe grows organically. Particularly important as a collaborative tool when both actors need to create a shared relational reality.
