Instrumental Preparation

Irreverent ActingImaging & UnconsciousBeing & DoingNo Acting Please
Work specifically designed to free the actor's personal instrument so that emotional blocks, inhibitions, fears, and conditioning can be eliminated, making the actor receptive to craft techniques. The 'missing link' in the Stanislavsky Method.

Stanislavsky's system did not recognize the need for instrumental preparation — which is why the Method failed for many actors. Actors who were naturally emotionally free succeeded with the Method; those with blocks could not apply it. Instrumental preparation must address the specific problems standing in the way of the actor's personal truth: fear, insecurity, behavioral conditioning, and social obligations.

Morris's first two books (No Acting Please and Being & Doing) focused almost exclusively on instrumental preparation. The domino effect: addressing one fear causes others to dissipate. Without instrumental preparation, even valid craft techniques are useless. It is the bridge between the Method's theory and its actual application.
