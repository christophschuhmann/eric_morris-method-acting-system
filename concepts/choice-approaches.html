Choice Approaches

Irreverent ActingUltimate ConsciousnessImaging & UnconsciousBeing & DoingNo Acting Please
The methods, techniques, and tools an actor actually uses when performing to make a chosen object or experience genuinely real — the 'how' to the choice's 'what.' The pick and shovel of the craft. While obligations and choices are intellectual identification areas, choice approaches are what the actor actually uses in the moment of acting.

Morris's system has evolved to include 27 choice approaches (Imaging = #26, Affective Memory = #12 in some enumerations; Irreverent Acting lists 22 functional tools). Multiple approaches exist for the same choice — the actor selects the approach best suited to the specific material situation.

The twenty-two (or more) choice approaches include: Sense Memory, Available Stimulus, Sensory Suggestion, Evocative Words, Believability, Selective Emphasis, Endowments, Imaginary Monologues, Imaginary Dialogues, Externals (Animals, People, Inanimate Objects, Insects), Objects that come into contact with the body, Affective Memory, Imaging, Inner Monologue, and others. Originally there were only four or five; the list grew as Morris's teaching evolved.

Selecting the right choice approach is a result of experience and experimentation. Some work far better in certain material situations. The actor must develop fluency across as many approaches as possible to have maximum craft flexibility.
