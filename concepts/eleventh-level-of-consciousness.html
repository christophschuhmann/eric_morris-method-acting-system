Eleventh Level of Consciousness

Imaging & UnconsciousIrreverent Acting
The state in which the actor is fully and simultaneously aware of their character's reality AND the actual performance environment (cues, other actors, blocking, audience) — a dual consciousness that allows organic character work without losing craft awareness. On the first ten levels the actor is totally in the flow of reality; on the eleventh, they retain awareness of being in performance.

This resolves the apparent contradiction between full organic immersion and theatrical craft responsibility. The actor says the proper words, doesn't actually strangle the leading lady, etc. — all from this eleventh level. It is a prerequisite for Programming the Unconscious: the actor must be sufficiently developed to maintain this dual awareness, or the risk of confusing imagined reality with performance reality becomes a psychological danger.

Without the eleventh level of consciousness, working with the unconscious for powerful or extreme roles carries real danger. It is the safety valve of the Morris system.
