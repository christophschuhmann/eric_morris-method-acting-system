Object Imaging Exercise Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Stretch and elevate the imagination by creating a complete sensory-rich story or life for an ordinary room object, developing the capacity for innovative, multi-sensory imaginative thinking.

How To

Part A — Story Object Imaging:

1. Select any object in the room.

2. Look at it; engage all five senses with it.

3. Begin imagining: Where was it before it was here? What environments did it inhabit? What would it say if it could speak? What does it prefer? What are its relationships to other objects in the room?

4. Develop a complete story. Let the imagination run free.

Part B — Impulsive Object Imaging:

5. Look at the same or another object.

6. Allow whatever image arises to appear — do not steer or edit.

7. Move from object to object, spending just moments on each.

8. Welcome bizarre, illogical, or outrageous images — these are signs of success.

9. Engage all five senses with each image even if it is a fleeting fragment.

Who Should Do It

All actors; especially those who are overly logical or who struggle to access their imagination spontaneously.

Why It Works

When logic is excluded, the imagination begins to tap into the unconscious — producing unexpected, rich, and emotionally resonant images. The exercise also trains all five senses to function as imagers.

Additional Info

Morris's example: lamp imaging — what did it look like as a baby lamp? Does it have parents? Painting imaging — where is each person in a Paris street scene going? What are they saying? This works imagination muscles the way physical exercise works muscles.
