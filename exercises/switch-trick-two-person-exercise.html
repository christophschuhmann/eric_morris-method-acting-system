Switch-Trick Exercise (Two-Person) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Drive an actor to the razor's edge of impulsivity, releasing congested emotional life from the unconscious by rapidly switching between stimuli.

How To

FACILITATOR (director/teacher): 1. Provide a single starting suggestion: 'Grade-school classroom.' 2. Wait a beat, then call 'Switch!' before the actor can settle into the image. 3. Continue calling 'Switch!' at varying intervals—sometimes so rapidly the actor seems to have no time to respond at all. 4. Inject your own suggestions periodically: a specific person, place, or experience. 5. Allow up to 10 minutes. ACTOR: 1. Respond to each suggestion/Switch with an immediate image. 2. All responses are in image form—not verbal explanations. 3. Sounds or words arise only from the images, not as commentary. 4. Expect nothing, judge nothing, premeditate nothing.

Who Should Do It

Actors in class or with a director/teacher. Best used as a preparation before scene work or as a regular class exercise.

Why It Works

The surprise and shock of rapid switching keeps the actor off balance, preventing all forms of preplanning. Congested emotional life that has been relegated to the unconscious has no time to be blocked and surfaces spontaneously.

Additional Info

After the exercise: actor typically feels adrenaline coursing, impulses bottlenecking, emotions converging and hard to separate. Even if the material that surfaces has nothing to do with the scene's obligations, the liberated unconscious life enriches everything that follows.

Related Technique: Disconnected Impulsivity Technique
