Imaging Environments Exercise (Relaxation) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Achieve a state of relaxation, involvement, and well-being through the creation of a beautiful or inspiring environment — real or imagined — at the start of a class, rehearsal, or before performance.

How To

1. Eyes closed or open; begin with a non-visual sense if possible (olfactory is a good start).

2. Begin imaging a place that has previously produced relaxation and well-being (ocean, forest, desert, mountains, home).

3. Expand through all five senses: smell, tactile, auditory, visual, gustatory.

4. Respond sensorially — NOT intellectually — in the specific sense organ.

5. Let the image deepen and expand organically.

6. Variations: image being buoyant in water (floating weightlessly in a dark tank); image rocking (while physically rocking to start the movement).

7. Continue until relaxation, involvement, and well-being are achieved.

Who Should Do It

All actors; particularly useful at the start of class or before performance when tension is present.

Why It Works

Any imaging that involves the actor takes him off the spot of self-consciousness. The environment image also directly produces physiological relaxation through the mind-body connection.

Additional Info

The environment selected should be personally meaningful and proven to produce well-being — Morris prefers natural environments. The rocking variant uses physical movement as an entry point; the movement can gradually be reduced as the image takes over.
