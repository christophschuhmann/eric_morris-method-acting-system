Primal Moan Exercise Instrumental

Imaging & UnconsciousUltimate ConsciousnessHollywood Stories
Goal

Crash through emotional blocks and solicit unconscious impulses and images by producing a deep, progressive moan from the fetal position.

How To

1. Assume the fetal position on the floor: curl into a ball, hands palms-together between thighs, like a fetus in the womb. 2. Begin a soft, deep moan from the lower abdomen. 3. Gradually build louder and deeper; hold each sound for an extended breath. 4. Ensure the sound vibrates from the lower body—not trapped in the throat. 5. Allow the sounds to embody emotional impulses as they arise: vulnerability, sadness, anger, rage. Let each emotion run its full course before yielding to the next. 6. When images arise, acknowledge them as unconscious communications—do not dismiss. 7. After establishing a flow of images, optionally suggest a place or time: e.g., 'one of your bedrooms from childhood.' Allow those suggested images to steer the unconscious into specific memory areas. 8. When complete, sit quietly for several moments of assimilation.

Who Should Do It

Actors who are carrying significant tension or anxiety into a demanding scene; actors entering a high-pressure set environment mid-production; actors facing emotionally vulnerable material or physical exposure.

Why It Works

The deep vibration and exhalation involved in sustained moaning physically releases held tension in the body; the permission to make primitive, non-judgmental sounds bypasses the self-conscious, socially controlled part of the actor and directly accesses the emotional instrument.

Additional Info

This exercise is inherently audible and cannot be done unobtrusively. It requires courage to do on a busy set. Jack Nicholson's supportive response (joining in from across the soundstage) represents the ideal collegial attitude toward another actor's instrument work.
