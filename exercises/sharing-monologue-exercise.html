Sharing Monologue Exercise Craft

Ultimate Consciousness
Goal

Generate a specific emotional state before or during a scene by speaking (aloud or silently) about real personal experiences that parallel the scene's emotional obligations.

How To

Identify the emotional obligation of the scene (e.g., depression, joy, grief, elation). Recall real experiences from your own life that contain or generated similar feelings. Speak about those experiences in detail, selectively emphasizing the elements that most powerfully generate the desired feeling. Can be done aloud before the scene or silently during it as an inner monologue.

Who Should Do It

All actors. Particularly accessible for beginners because it doesn't require complex sense memory — just honest sharing of real experience.

Why It Works

Real experiences carry real feelings. By selectively mining one's own history for parallel material, the actor creates genuine emotional life that directly addresses the scene's obligations.

Additional Info

The selective emphasis is key — don't tell everything, choose the elements that most powerfully generate the desired state. Can transition seamlessly into an inner monologue during the scene itself.
