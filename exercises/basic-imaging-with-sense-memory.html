Basic Imaging with Sense Memory Exercise Both

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Learn to create a dimensionally real environment or event by combining inner visualization with systematic sensorial questioning, training all five senses to respond simultaneously.

How To

1. Do a relaxation exercise to clear intrusive thoughts.

2. Begin with an inner-eye (eyes-closed) vision of a place or event — sketch in overall elements (trees, lake, mountains, sky).

3. Open eyes and transfer the image to outer vision (image with eyes open).

4. Begin asking sensorial questions directed at specific sense organs:
- Visual: 'How many colors do I see? How do they blend?'
- Tactile: 'Where on my face do I feel the wind? What does it feel like?'
- Olfactory: 'What is the exact odor? Where in my nose do I detect it?'
- Auditory: 'What is the most noticeable sound? Where does it come from?'
- Gustatory: 'Can I taste any of the odors? Where in my mouth?'

5. Allow images to run free between sensorial questions — the image itself suggests what to explore next.

6. Intersperse imaging (free flow) with sensorial questions to deepen and dimensionalize the experience.

Who Should Do It

All actors learning the imaging system; beginners to Sense Memory; any actor wanting to deepen the tangibility of their choices.

Why It Works

Sense Memory responses happen in the body's actual sense organs — they create genuine physiological and emotional reactions. Combining with imaging keeps the experience flowing and prevents it from becoming purely analytical.

Additional Info

Morris gives two detailed examples: (1) a mountain lake scene (pp. 40-41), (2) a visit to the Metropolitan Museum of Art (pp. 43-44). The museum example shows how imaging can re-create real past events sensorially, producing emotional life appropriate for a scene obligation.
