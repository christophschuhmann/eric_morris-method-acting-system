Available Stimulus Exploration (Craft Use) Craft

Irreverent Acting
Goal

Identify which elements in the actual environment can be used to fulfill scene obligations before resorting to other choice approaches.

How To

Go through a systematic checklist of questions: How am I affected by the whole place? As I scan the environment, what affects or changes my emotional point of view? Are there sounds or odors that mean anything to me? What about the furnishings, colors, arrangement, light? How do I feel about individual people collectively? Individually? Do any lines or words in the play mean something to me? Does anything in the play, the actors, or the place remind me of something in my life? Use Inner Monologue to intensify whatever Available Stimulus affects you.

Who Should Do It

Any actor approaching a scene, before selecting other choice approaches.

Why It Works

Available Stimulus is the most efficiently accessible approach—when it works, it requires the least additional craft work. What is already there and real doesn't need to be created.

Additional Info

Checklist can be expanded as the actor works with the approach. Sometimes a specific element needs Creative Manipulation to be fully useful.
