Believability Preparation Exercise Instrumental

Ultimate Consciousness
Goal

Build an ensemble foundation by starting with real truths between the actors and gradually incorporating the fictional elements of the material.

How To

Identify what is truly real between the two actors that parallels the material (e.g., a real mutual attraction if the scene is a romantic encounter). Begin the improvisation starting from that true foundation. Express the real feelings honestly. Then add fictional elements from the scene or character one by one. Sprinkle the fiction into the truth rather than replacing it.

Who Should Do It

All actors doing two-person scene work; most powerful when there are genuine emotional parallels between the actors' real lives and the scene's content.

Why It Works

Real feelings provide an authentic anchor; the vested interest created by genuine mutual engagement elevates moment-to-moment involvement and creates a natural ensemble dependency.

Additional Info

Most enjoyable of all ensemble preparations. Allows actors to express real feelings they've been reluctant to expose. Start with what is true — the fiction becomes believable when grounded in truth.
