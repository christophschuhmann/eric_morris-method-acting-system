Romances, Relationships, Sexuality (Unconscious Suggestions) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Re-create significant romantic relationships and attractions using Free-Association or Fragmented Imaging to liberate the powerful unconscious emotional material stored in those experiences.

How To

1. Start with a name and face—it need not be your earliest attraction. 2. Use Free-Association Imaging: 'Doris! Blue-and-white checkered dress...' Allow images to evolve impulsively from the name. 3. Use all five senses simultaneously: visual, auditory, olfactory, tactile, gustatory. 4. Allow the people in the images to blend together; do not derail by commenting. 5. Accept mixed sensory responses (visual from one person, auditory from another). 6. Stay with each person until images run out or another face appears. 7. Allow all ensuing emotions (yearning, pain, joy, anger, confusion) to be expressed. 8. After completing: do inventory of emotional responses and note unconscious impulses.

Who Should Do It

All actors; particularly valuable for those needing to access romantic, sexual, or longing emotional states.

Why It Works

Some of the strongest emotions in human life surround love and attraction. Many memories in this area are assigned to the unconscious, especially unresolved feelings from volatile or ended relationships. Volatile relationships carry unexpressed emotions that surface when the relationship is re-imaged.

Additional Info

Morris's example: imaging Doris (sixth-grade crush) produces a taste of attraction/desire, a familiar yearning with pain attached, then other faces blending in. After his second divorce: dreams continued for months, waking him angry.

Related Technique: Free Association Imaging Technique
