Imaginary Monologue Combined with Sense Memory Craft

Irreverent Acting
Goal

Dimensionalize an Imaginary Monologue by adding sensory questions about the imaginary person in between the words, allowing the person to appear to respond and creating unpredictability in the expression.

How To

Begin establishing the imaginary person sensorially: shape of face, color of eyes, distance, posture. Then begin the monologue, pausing at natural points to ask sensory questions about what the person is doing or showing in response to what was just said. Example sequence: 'How far away is he standing?...How tall is he?...What is the shape of his face?...' then begin words, then mid-sentence ask 'What is his mouth like?...How is he looking at me as I talk to him?' then continue words. The sensory questions can begin before the monologue words and continue after the monologue ends. Allow the imaginary person's responses to genuinely affect what is said next.

Who Should Do It

Any actor using Imaginary Monologue as a choice approach who wants to deepen the reality and create genuine unpredictability.

Why It Works

Seeing the person you are talking to allows for their responses to be perceived—the imaginary person becomes more real and more reactive, producing less predictable and more organic emotional expression.

Additional Info

A very natural combination: the two approaches support each other rather than interfering. Sense Memory part can start before the monologue and continue after it ends.
