Logy Exercise (Hypnagogic Induction) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Deliberately induce the hypnagogic state to access unconscious images while maintaining enough consciousness to be aware of them.

How To

1. Lie in a prone position on the floor or on a comfortable surface. 2. Begin to exaggerate your body weight—imagine you weigh five thousand pounds. 3. Make a sustained sound using the word 'logy' continuously through the exercise. 4. Allow yourself to feel the weight deeply. Try rolling to your side to experience the enormous effort that requires. 5. As the 'logy' feeling deepens, approach the sleep threshold. 6. When you feel yourself beginning to 'drop off,' stop the sound and encourage images. 7. Stay semi-conscious: be aware of the images as they arise. 8. Trick: when the hypnagogic episode ends (you startle awake), immediately re-enter the logy state and try again. 9. Duration: up to 30 minutes. 10. Afterward: move directly into working for a choice for the material.

Who Should Do It

All actors seeking unconscious access. Can be done in a rehearsal lobby, at home, or anywhere with sufficient privacy. Best results come after regular repetition over time.

Why It Works

The hypnagogic state is the moment when the unconscious most actively communicates with consciousness through images. By deliberately and repeatedly approaching this threshold, the actor learns to 'work' the unconscious in a semi-awake state.

Additional Info

Risk: falling fully asleep (which also happens and is acceptable). Morris sends students to the theater lobby to do this—they return looking as if they had just awakened from impacting dreams: permissive, benevolent, ready to work.
