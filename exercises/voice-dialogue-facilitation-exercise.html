Voice Dialogue Facilitation (Shirley Example) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Develop an aware ego and map the subject's subpersonality structure by speaking directly with each self as it emerges.

How To

FACILITATOR: 1. Seat yourself facing the subject. Begin: 'Tell me what's going on in your life.' 2. Note vocal and physical changes as different selves emerge. 3. When a new energy appears, immediately address it: 'Who are you?' Identify the self by its characteristics (tone, posture, attitude). 4. Talk to each self as a distinct entity; do not disagree or engage in conflict. 5. Periodically move the subject to a new space to access the aware ego: 'I'd like to speak to Shirley now.' 6. In the aware-ego state, recap observations and share conclusions. 7. End by returning to a primary self. SUBJECT: - Physically move to a different area of the room for each self. - Each self refers to the subject in third person ('her,' not 'me'). - Allow the self's energy to transform your posture, voice, and attitude fully.

Who Should Do It

All actors as the primary prerequisite to any subpersonality acting work. Also valuable for personal growth and self-awareness independent of acting.

Why It Works

By addressing each self as a distinct entity, the facilitator allows it to express itself fully without being censored by the controlling selves. The physical movement between spaces reinforces that each self occupies a different energetic territory. Over time, the aware ego grows stronger and can hold all selves in balance.

Additional Info

An average session lasts one hour or more. Shirley's example: protector/controller emerged quickly and revealed the child history of verbal abuse; shy child and pleaser were also identified; rebellious child broke through at school age. The aware ego described itself as 'just coming into being' – evidence that it develops progressively through repeated voice dialogue.
