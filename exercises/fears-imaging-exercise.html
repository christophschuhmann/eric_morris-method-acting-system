Fears Imaging Exercise Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Access unconscious material rooted in conscious fears by imaging the fears in full sensory detail or by incubating them silently.

How To

1. Begin by articulating all fears you are aware of: 'I'm afraid of dying... I'm afraid of water where I can't see the bottom... I'm afraid of pain... I'm afraid of being laughed at...' 2. Choose one fear and either: (A) IMAGE IT: Create the full sensory manifestation of the fear with all five senses. 'I'm afraid of dying' → image yourself expiring or connected to a respirator. 'I'm afraid of being laughed at' → create an entire room of people laughing. (B) INCUBATE IT: Do not image anything. Sit with the fear, let it run its course. Find out what the fear means to you personally. 3. Allow whatever unconscious material the fear surfaces to fully emerge. 4. Repeat with other fears. Repeat the exercise often—new fears surface each time.

Who Should Do It

All actors seeking to deepen their connection to the unconscious. Particularly useful for those whose work feels shallow or lacks emotional depth.

Why It Works

Our greatest fears are often denied and pushed into unconscious recesses. Facing them directly through imaging or incubation releases the associated unconscious material and makes it available for creative use.

Additional Info

Morris's own list of fears includes: water where he can't see the bottom, terminal disease, pain, the unknown, high places, falling, strange places, needles, being alone, loneliness.
