Multi-Approach Imaging Rehearsal: Waiting for Lefty (Joe and Edna) Craft

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Demonstrate how multiple choice approaches—Available Stimulus, Endowments, Affective Memory, Believability, Imaginary Dialogues—can all be combined with Imaging as the craft technique in a sustained rehearsal process.

How To

1. Begin with Available Stimulus: explore real feelings between the two actors using Selective Emphasis. 2. Each actor selects a personal parallel relationship (a real person) as their choice. 3. Use Endowments with Fragmented Imaging + Imaging Sensations to sculpt the choice person onto the scene partner (conglomerate endowment). 4. Augment with Believability + Imaginary Dialogues: improvise a parallel scenario in which both actors speak to their choices while responding to each other. 5. Use Story or Fragmented Imaging for Affective Memory explorations of specific episodes from each actor's real relationship with their choice. 6. Both actors create the time-and-place environment for the scene through Imaging before each rehearsal. 7. Over multiple rehearsals, the combination of all approaches produces the five-year intimacy and complex ambivalence of Joe and Edna.

Who Should Do It

Advanced actors and directors working on relationship-heavy scenes requiring the depth of a long history that cannot be produced by a single technique alone.

Why It Works

No single choice approach can replicate all dimensions of a complex long-term relationship. The combination of approaches addresses different facets: Available Stimulus establishes present-tense truth; Endowments create the specific person; Affective Memory supplies emotional history; Believability fills gaps with parallel truth; Imaginary Dialogues make the text feel real.

Additional Info

This case study is Morris's most complex example of Imaging in rehearsal. It shows that Imaging is not a single tool but an entire approach system that can interconnect with virtually every other choice approach in the system.
