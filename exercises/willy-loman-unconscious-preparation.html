Unconscious Preparation for Willy Loman (Death of a Salesman) Craft

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Identify and use specific unconscious preparations (parallel life experiences) to build the emotional foundation for Willy Loman's psychology of desperate achievement-seeking and delusional self-image.

How To

1. Analyze Willy's unconscious underpinnings: unrealistic expectations, need to compete and achieve, gap between dreams and reality. 2. Take an inventory of your own experiences where great demands were placed on you to accomplish, compete, be successful. 3. Use Imaging to re-create each such experience, selectively emphasizing only those that produced the underpinnings of need-to-achieve. 4. Morris's personal parallel: growing up with siblings 11-20 years older; expectations from birth; psychological abuse from oldest brother; self-induced intellectual blockage; being sent to a psychologist; discovering his intelligence had been suppressed by trauma. 5. Structure a chronological storyboard of these events; image them repeatedly. 6. Encourage dreams related to themes of achievement, failure, competition. 7. Before addressing scene obligations: the unconscious foundation is laid. Then address obligations scene by scene.

Who Should Do It

Actors working on Willy Loman, or characters driven by compulsive achievement needs, delusional thinking, or a history of suppressed potential.

Why It Works

Willy Loman's psychology has unconscious roots in experiences of humiliation and inadequacy that drive compensatory behavior. By re-creating your own parallel experiences of those forces, you build an organic unconscious foundation for the character's distorted perception of himself and the world.

Additional Info

Morris notes: it is not that the actor IS like the character—it is that by selectively isolating and emphasizing the parallel experiences, they create an emotional DNA that mirrors the character's. Quote: 'I'm not John Jones, but I am! Everybody is!'
