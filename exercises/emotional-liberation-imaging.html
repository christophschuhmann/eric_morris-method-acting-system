Emotional Liberation Imaging (Anger/Conflict) Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Free the actor from inhibitions around expressing extreme emotions (anger, rage, conflict, vulnerability, grief) by creating Story Images that express the repressed emotion fully and successfully.

How To

1. Identify the specific emotional block (e.g., fear of anger, conflict phobia, inability to express grief).

2. Using Story Imaging with Sense Memory, create the environment where the emotion was originally suppressed — the specific place, the people involved, the sensory details.

3. Begin to re-experience the original scene sensorially.

4. Now, change the story: express the suppressed emotion fully — scream at the parent, stand up to the bully, confront the intimidator.

5. Image the changed outcome — the other person's response of shame, understanding, or capitulation; your own sense of power and righteousness.

6. Create an image of resolution and possibly connection after the confrontation.

7. Repeat daily across different confrontation scenarios and with different people from your life.

Who Should Do It

Actors who find specific emotional expressions blocked or uncomfortable; especially those with conflict phobia or unexpressed anger.

Why It Works

The inhibition is rooted in specific memories and unconscious structures built around suppressed experience. By sensorially re-creating the original scenario and changing the outcome, the actor accesses and reprograms the unconscious structure that maintains the inhibition.

Additional Info

Morris's example (pp. 116-117): angry child confronting unreasonable father in Story Imaging; the father is moved to shame and love; the outcome is resolution. Note: the image should be structured so all senses respond to each element.
