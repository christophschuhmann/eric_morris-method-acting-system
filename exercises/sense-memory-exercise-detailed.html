Sense Memory (Full Process) Craft

Irreverent Acting
Goal

Create an absent object, person, or place so fully that the actor can actually see, hear, smell, feel, and taste it—making it real enough to stimulate authentic emotional responses.

How To

Begin with the first sensory question directed to a specific part of one specific sense. For example (visual): 'What color are the walls of this place? How does the light affect the color?' Respond through the sense—actually attempt to see the answer, not just think it. Ask many questions per sense area (can be 50+). Leave and return to a sense as needed. Work all five senses: visual, tactile, olfactory, auditory, gustatory. Go back and forth between senses while sustaining previously created sensory impressions. The process continues during scene work—between lines and while listening to other actor.

Who Should Do It

All actors as a daily practice; anyone using Sense Memory as a choice approach in scene work.

Why It Works

The senses can be trained through daily practice to respond to absent objects as if present. Not an intellectual but a response process.

Additional Info

Daily practice is essential—the ability to truly see/feel/smell absent objects comes with sustained practice over time, not immediately.
