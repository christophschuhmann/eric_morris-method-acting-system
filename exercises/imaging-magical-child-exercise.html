Imaging a Subpersonality: The Magical Child Both

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Access the Magical Child subpersonality through Morris's four-part imaging process and inhabit it fully enough to carry it into material.

How To

1. Relax. Choose a physical space in the room where the Magical Child's energy feels located. 2. Begin imaging the child sensorially: - How far away? How tall? Hair color? Face shape? - Skin texture, laughter quality, eyes wide with wonder, excitement. - See happiness exuding from every pore; wonder and curiosity in the gaze. - All imagery is sensory – no verbal or intellectual involvement. 3. When the child feels tangible and real, move into that exact space. Allow yourself to STEP INTO THAT ENERGY FIELD AND BECOME THAT ENERGY. Encourage your entire being to merge with the magical child. 4. Find a mirror. Look into it. Observe physical changes: face, eyes, posture. Stay until you see and feel the magical child's presence in your reflection. Return to the mirror if you lose the connection. 5. From the magical child's perspective, relate to the environment: - Image imaginary objects, people, experiences the child would create. - Let the imaging continue for 8-10 minutes. 6. Gently move toward the text: 'at the eleventh level of consciousness,' relate to the available stimuli (other actors) from the magical child's energy, or image things directly related to the material.

Who Should Do It

Actors playing characters with magical child energy or who need childlike wonder, openness, and excitement in their work. Also a good entry point for all actors new to subpersonality imaging.

Why It Works

The sensory specificity of imaging the subpersonality creates a tangible, embodied presence rather than a concept. Stepping into the space grounds the energy in the body. Mirror work verifies and deepens the embodiment by providing visual feedback of the transformation.

Additional Info

Each subpersonality has a distinctive look: magical child differs from vulnerable child; rebellious child may be bigger and more muscular; protector may look like General Patton. Primary selves are most accessible; disowned selves require the most experience.
