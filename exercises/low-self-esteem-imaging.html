Low Self-Esteem Imaging Instrumental

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Build a new, positive, emotionally grounded self-image by creating progressive story images of appreciation, admiration, and confident self-expression.

How To

1. Begin by imaging yourself as you truly feel you are inside — the best version of yourself physically, mentally, and emotionally.

2. Image yourself in an attitude of attractive power: strong, bright, articulate, creative.

3. Optionally, re-create events that contributed to the low self-esteem, then progressively modify those images.

4. Bring others into the image; allow yourself to feel you deserve their admiration.

5. Image yourself in situations where you would normally feel inadequate — now image yourself thriving.

6. Repeat daily, creating variations each time.

7. Keep a journal of which specific images have the greatest unconscious impact.

Who Should Do It

Actors with fragile egos, low self-esteem, or persistent feelings of inadequacy.

Why It Works

The self-images planted in the unconscious during formative years replay automatically; replacing them with new images through daily imaging progressively builds a new unconscious self-structure.

Additional Info

Morris notes: 'Inside that heavy young girl is a beautiful and thin young woman!' — the imaging reveals and reinforces the inner self that already exists.
