Relationship Preparation via Endowment + Guided Imaging (Night Mother Example) Craft

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Build a deeply authentic mother-daughter relationship for a scene using a combination of real-life experiences, endowment imaging, guided imaging, and shared imaging.

How To

Step 1 — Establish available realities: Note real similarities between the actors and the characters.
Step 2 — Relate as yourselves: Express moment-to-moment real feelings to each other; build a foundation of truth.
Step 3 — Live together (or spend extended time together): Share real-life experiences, habits, conversations.
Step 4 — Shared Imaging: Share personal experiences from your own life that parallel the play's circumstances.
Step 5 — Endowment imaging: Each actress talks to the other while imaging her own real mother/daughter in the other's place. Blend the image with the actual responses received.
Step 6 — Guided Imaging: Facilitate each other through personal experiences that closely parallel scenes in the play.
Step 7 — Simultaneous Guided Imaging: Both select parallel experiences and mutually facilitate each other through them.
Step 8 — Parallel Imaging (individual): Each actress images herself having the character's life and experiences.

Who Should Do It

Actors in two-person plays with deep relationship obligations; applicable to any scene requiring intimate, historically grounded relationships.

Why It Works

Multiple layers of real experience, imaging parallels, and guided exploration cumulatively build an unconscious relationship that feels lived-in — not performed.

Additional Info

This was Morris's preparation for 'night, Mother (Marsha Norman). The play has no intermission and only two characters; the relationship is everything. Morris also had the actresses literally live together during rehearsal.
