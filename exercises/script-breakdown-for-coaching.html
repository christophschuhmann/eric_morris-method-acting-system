Script Breakdown for Acting Coaching Both

Hollywood Stories
Goal

For a coach working with an actor on unfamiliar material or a specific role, to break the script down scene by scene and identify the acting obligations, character needs, and moments requiring special attention before coaching begins.

How To

The coach reads the script thoroughly, identifying: (1) what each scene requires emotionally and behaviorally; (2) where the character's relationships with other characters must be felt specifically; (3) what instrumental and craft tools may be needed; (4) which scenes require special preparation. Then works with the actor scene by scene, addressing instrumental work before craft work.

Who Should Do It

Acting coaches working with actors preparing for specific productions, particularly when the coach has limited time (as Morris had nine weeks with Schwarzenegger).

Why It Works

Scene-by-scene breakdown prevents the actor from arriving at emotionally demanding moments unprepared; allows coach to target instrumental work precisely where it is needed.

Additional Info

Morris describes this when working with Schwarzenegger: 'When we finally got to the script, I broke it down scene by scene.' He was initially honest with the director that nine weeks was very short time and that he might confuse Arnold rather than help him.
