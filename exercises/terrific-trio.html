The Terrific Trio Instrumental

Freeing the Actor
Goal

Break through very intense tension walls through a three-part sequence of physical tantrum, large vocal expurgation, and active exorcism.

How To

Three parts done consecutively: (1) Abandonment — lie on back and have a full vocal and physical tantrum (yelling, bellowing, flailing arms and legs) until some exhaustion arrives. (2) Vesuvius — stand and vocalize all suppressed feelings loudly and fully, even starting arbitrarily; let real emotions connect as they will. (3) Exorcism — shout 'Get out! Get out! Get out!' while physically thrusting both arms forward violently, directing the expulsion at real people, situations, and things. Continue until liberated.

Who Should Do It

Actors with very large tension issues when other relaxation approaches have not worked. Can be done alone at home or in a class setting.

Why It Works

The intensity of the physical and vocal release cracks the 'walls' of intense tension. Even arbitrary initiation quickly connects to real emotional material. Exhaustion from physical tantrum releases somatic holding.

Additional Info

Morris describes using a 'mini abandonment' in an audition restroom stall — making less noise but getting the physical release — which he credits with getting a part. Abandonment alone is sometimes enough to break the tension wall.
