The Cluster of Four Instrumental

No Acting Please
Goal

An ideally balanced group of four exercises to begin any class, rehearsal, or work session. Systematically addresses tension, awareness, feeling, and desire.

How To

In sequence: (1) Relaxation exercise (Logey, Tense and Relax, Deep Breathing, Rag Doll, or similar); (2) Sensitizing — raising your senses to higher levels of sensitivity; (3) Personal Inventory — finding out how you feel; (4) What Do I Want — as part of Personal Inventory. The sequence is not accidental: address physical tension first so you can sensitize, then find out how you feel and what you want.

Who Should Do It

Any actor at the start of a work session. Used in class every week for years as the standard opening exercise.

Why It Works

The sequence is carefully ordered: relaxation enables sensitizing; sensitizing enables honest Personal Inventory; Personal Inventory grounds the actor in reality before creative work begins.

Additional Info

Morris used this to start every class for years. Sensuality exercise works well following the Cluster of Four.
