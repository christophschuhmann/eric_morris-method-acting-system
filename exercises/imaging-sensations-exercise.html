Imaging Sensations Exercise Both

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Train all five senses to function as equal, simultaneous imagers — overcoming visual dominance and building sensory accessibility across all channels.

How To

1. Do a relaxation exercise.

2. Choose an image (real or fantasy).

3. Begin with a non-visual sense (smell or sound) to deliberately counter the habit of starting visually.

4. Allow each sense to respond with full emphasis — the same total engagement as you would give the visual sense.

5. Shift emphasis organically from sense to sense: hear the image, smell it, feel it, taste it, see it — in any order.

6. Let all senses blend and respond simultaneously as the image deepens.

7. No sensorial questions — allow each sense to respond fully and continuously.

Who Should Do It

Actors who are visually dominant in their imaging; anyone training in Morris's system who wants to deepen sensory accessibility.

Why It Works

By giving equal billing to all senses, the actor creates a more complete internal reality. The image becomes a total body experience, not just a mental picture, making it more convincing to the unconscious and more emotionally impacting.

Additional Info

Morris's fantasy example (bright-red/orange ground, rainbow-colored forest, speaking creatures) illustrates how even a completely invented image produces real emotional impact when all senses are fully engaged. (Pages 46-49)
