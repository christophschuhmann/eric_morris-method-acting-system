Involuntary Imaging Exercise (Class Format) Craft

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Access deeper, unconsciously mediated emotional material by working for a choice to stimulate involuntary images, then re-creating those images as a richer substitute approach.

How To

Part 1 (approx. 15 minutes):

1. Identify a specific obligation from a scene (current or past).

2. Select a choice that would stimulate the desired emotional life.

3. Work for the choice using any choice approach.

4. While working, be acutely aware of all involuntary images that arise — no matter how abstract or seemingly unrelated.

5. Take note (mental or written) of as many as possible.

Part 2:

6. Recall the involuntary images from Part 1.

7. Re-create those images using Imaging as a choice approach.

8. Notice how the emotional life from re-creating the involuntary images compares to the original choice.

Who Should Do It

Intermediate to advanced actors with some imaging experience; those wanting to access deeper emotional material and build a connection with their unconscious.

Why It Works

The involuntary images are the unconscious's own translation of the conscious choice — connected to the obligation in the unconscious's pictorial language, and re-creating them produces richer, less intellectually filtered emotional life.

Additional Info

Morris reports that in class, nearly all actors found the involuntary images produced more impacting emotional life than their original choices, even when the images seemed abstractly unrelated to the scene material.
