Two-People BEING Instrumental

No Acting Please
Goal

A four-part preparation exercise to establish BEING with a partner, get related, begin sensory work, and prepare for scene work.

How To

Part 1: Both actors semi-audibly express all anxieties and tensions about being in front of the group in stream-of-consciousness style. Part 2: Observe-Wonder-Perceive your partner. Part 3: Work for a simple sensory choice in relation to your partner without obligating to any result (e.g., create eyeglasses on partner, different hair color, specific look in eyes). Part 4: If preparing for a scene, begin working for your first choice in that scene; if just a BEING exercise, obligate yourself to something you want to feel toward your partner (jealousy, desire, compassion, fear) and work sensorily for it. Throughout, acknowledge and express anything that intrudes on your awareness.

Who Should Do It

Any two actors who are about to rehearse together, do a scene, or audition together. Also valuable as a daily BEING practice.

Why It Works

Addresses tension about performing for others, gets partners truly involved with each other rather than performing, and transitions naturally into choice work for the scene.

Additional Info

Important to remain standing throughout — intensifies the feeling of being on the spot. Make the obligation specific: not general jealousy but what you are jealous about specifically.
