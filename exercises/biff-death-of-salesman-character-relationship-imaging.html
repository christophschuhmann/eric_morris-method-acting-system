Character Relationship Imaging: Death of a Salesman (Biff and Willy) Craft

Imaging & Unconscious
Goal

Build the complex emotional history of a character relationship using Fragmented Imaging + Imaging Sensations across multiple selectively emphasized life episodes.

How To

1. Identify the most complex relationship in the play (Biff → Willy). 2. Select a personal choice: your own father (or equivalent figure) as the parallel. 3. Start with Available Stimulus: look for any similarities between your scene partner and your father (face, mannerisms, voice, rhythm). 4. Begin Fragmented Imaging of selectively emphasized episodes from childhood to present: - Baseball field (age 6): father's anger, tobacco breath, face in yours. - Family dinner table (age 10): father boasting, looking at you disapprovingly. - Graduation ceremony: father's arm around you, public pride and pressure. 5. Use Imaging Sensations as the process within each episode: all five senses simultaneously. 6. Cover 10-15 specific events; move to next when current feels complete. 7. Optionally supplement with Story Imaging or Fantasy Imaging of additional events.

Who Should Do It

Actors playing characters in deeply complex, ambivalent, or historically layered relationships. Especially useful when the written relationship has years of history prior to the play's action.

Why It Works

Selectively emphasizing experiences that mirror the script's relationship dynamics creates an authentic emotional history the actor can draw on throughout the run. Building this across multiple episodes (not just one memory) produces the complexity and ambivalence of a real long-term relationship.

Additional Info

The Available Stimulus exploration of the scene partner simultaneously helps establish the Endowment foundation—if the actor later uses Endowments, the preliminary similarities work is already done.
